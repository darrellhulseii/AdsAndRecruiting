Step 0 · one-time

Before a single dollar runs

These happen once. Paid spend stays paused until all six are done.

  1. Landing page + lead form approved by PV Marketing. Submit a PDF mockup or private URL. Confirm the privacy policy and opt-in checkbox sit next to the form.
  2. Campaign assets approved by PV. Keyword list, the PV negative-keyword list, and all ad copy submitted and approved before going live.
  3. Direct Protection Security ad rules confirmed in writing — brand use and allowed claims.
  4. Tracking installed. Conversion tracking on the lead form, call tracking on click-to-call, and UTM tags so every lead maps to an ad.
  5. Geo-targeting set to serviceable areas only — Inland Empire, and South Bay / Gilroy / Morgan Hill / Monterey. Never pay for clicks you cannot sell.
  6. Lead routing live. Every form submit instantly pings the rep so consented leads get called back fast.

The daily routine

What you do every day

Three short blocks, plus the one rule that moves the needle more than anything else.

08:00 — 09:00

☀️ Morning

  • Call every consented lead from overnight first — speed wins.
  • Check pacing: did yesterday land on budget? Is today pacing right?
  • Scan search terms; add junk and brand terms to negatives.
  • Pause any ad or keyword that spent yesterday with zero leads.
12:00 — 13:00

🕛 Midday

  • Respond to every new lead since morning.
  • Shift a little budget toward the morning's best performer.
  • Check social ad frequency; queue fresh creative if it is climbing.
17:00 — 19:00

🌆 Evening · primetime

  • Highest-intent window — make sure a winner is not budget-capped.
  • Fastest payoff on lead response; people are home and reachable.
  • Log spend, leads, and cost per lead. Note tomorrow's one fix.
5min

Call every paid lead within 5 minutes

The single biggest lever you have. The lead-form opt-in is your consent to call or text, which keeps the call-back compliant — and Frontier and EarthLink phone sales are inbound and consent-driven only, so paid leads must come through the consented form or a click-to-call.

Operator playbook

Advanced paid plays

How the best paid marketers — Gary Vaynerchuk, Alex Hormozi, and the current Meta playbook — actually run ads now. Same compliance rules as the rest of the plan.

Creative is the new targeting Gary Vee + Meta
The creative, not the audience, now decides who you reach and whether it works. Feed broad targeting and Meta's AI a high volume of strong creative and let it find the buyers.
Run it: run 10-15 short vertical clips per campaign on broad targeting; let the algorithm sort it; refresh by swapping just the first three seconds.
Day trading attention Gary Vaynerchuk
Treat paid like a trading desk: fast creative testing, daily measurement, and cultural timing. If an ad flops, it is almost always the creative, not the platform.
Run it: launch small, read the numbers within days, kill losers fast, and move budget to winners every week.
Volume and thumb-stopping hooks Gary Vaynerchuk
Make enough creative, edit it native to each platform, and win the first three seconds or lose the view. One idea per ad.
Run it: batch-film at the door, cut many hooks from one shoot, and test a new opening line against the same video body.
The grand slam offer Alex Hormozi
Make the offer so good people feel stupid saying no: stack real value, add a guarantee, add a reason to act now. A great offer beats a clever ad.
Run it: lead with a genuinely strong offer — free availability and speed check, free home-security assessment, or an install perk — not just "get internet."
Lead magnets that convert Alex Hormozi
Give away something valuable that solves one narrow problem and points to your core offer. Make it good enough to charge for, and build several versions to rotate.
Run it: offer a free address speed-and-availability report, a home-security checklist, or an ISP bill audit, captured through your consented form.
Prove it, then pour fuel Alex Hormozi
There are only four ways to get leads: warm outreach, cold outreach, content, and paid ads. Prove the offer and angle with organic first, then scale the winner with paid.
Run it: find the hook that already performs in your organic clips, then put ad budget behind that exact angle.
Consolidate, do not fragment Meta 2026
The algorithm needs data to learn, so one larger campaign beats five tiny ones. Keep the structure simple: one prospecting campaign plus one retargeting campaign.
Run it: put all your best creative in a single broad prospecting campaign rather than splitting budget across many ad sets.
Retarget warm audiences Meta 2026
People who already watched your videos or visited the site convert far cheaper than cold traffic. Always run a dedicated retargeting layer.
Run it: send 15-20% of budget to video viewers and site visitors from the last 7-30 days with a direct "check your address" ad.
Low-friction lead forms Meta 2026
Instant lead forms cut fill time from minutes to seconds and generate more leads, while Pixel and Conversions API feed the signal back so delivery improves.
Run it: use instant forms that keep your consent box and privacy policy, sync leads to your inbox instantly, and respond within five minutes.

Step-by-step: how to spend it
Week 1 — Test ~60% daily
Spend light while you learn. Run several hooks against one offer in one format. You are buying data, not sales yet.
Run it: do not scale; just find your two cheapest hooks by cost per lead.
Week 2 — Validate ~90% daily
Take the winning hooks and confirm they hold up. Add your best format. Watch cost per lead and lead quality.
Run it: kill anything above your target cost per lead.
Week 3 — Scale ~120% daily
Pour budget into the proven hook and format. Turn on retargeting. Keep one small test running on the side.
Run it: raise budget gradually so the algorithm keeps learning.
Week 4 — Scale+ ~130% daily
Push the winners harder and test a fresh angle or geo. Lock in your cost per sale and ROAS for the month.
Run it: refresh creative before fatigue sets in.

Creative-testing schedule
Week 1 — Hooks Biggest lever
Run 5-8 different opening lines with everything else identical. The first three seconds decide reach. Keep the top two by cost per lead.
Run it: one variable only: the hook.
Week 2 — Formats Find the winner
Winning hook, new formats: talking-head, doorstep UGC, text-on-screen, carousel. See which converts cheapest.
Run it: hold the hook constant; only the format changes.
Week 3 — Offers Sharpen the ask
Winning hook and format, 2-3 offers: free availability check vs free report vs install perk. Let cost per lead decide.
Run it: the offer often beats the ad — test it deliberately.
Week 4 — Audience and scale Lock it in
Winning combo; test geo radius and placements, then move budget onto the champions.
Run it: graduate winners; retire anything fatigued.

Refresh creative every 2-3 weeks to fight fatigue, and always keep one new test running.

No ad spend

The organic playbook

Earn attention, trust, and leads without paying for them — built on how the best operators and companies actually grew. Same compliance rules as paid: generic on social.

Ryan Magin Short-form / TikTok
Cast a wide net with broad content, then repurpose relentlessly. Win the first three seconds with a hook, and edit native to each platform — what works on TikTok isn't what works elsewhere.
Run it: film one shoot at the door, cut it into 5-10 clips. Open every video with a hook — "most homes on this street can get fiber and don't know it." Post daily; let volume surface the winners.
Gary Vaynerchuk Attention / brand
His "jab, jab, jab, right hook" rule: give value far more often than you ask, so the ask lands. "Day trading attention" means making platform-native content in today's underpriced channels — and you're only as good as your last post.
Run it: document, don't just create. Give value 80% of the time (speed tips, security tips, neighborhood updates) so the 20% "check your address" ask actually converts.
Top agencies' pages Brand / proof
The best agencies use their own social to lead with the work as proof — results, real campaigns, testimonials — broadcast a strong point of view, and show their culture and people, consistently and in a clear voice.
Run it: post real installs and wins, customer testimonials, and behind-the-scenes team culture. Make Complete Home Today a recognizable local brand, not a faceless knock.
Steve Jobs Story / simplicity
"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication." — Steve Jobs
Sell dreams, not products — lead with the outcome people want, not the spec sheet. Start with the customer experience and work back to the offer.
Run it: don't sell "symmetrical fiber" — sell the connected, frustration-free home and peace of mind. One simple idea per post. Experience first, tech second.
Elon Musk Product / word of mouth
"Awesome products grow with word of mouth." — Elon Musk
Reason from first principles — question how door-to-door "has to" be done — and make the experience so good it spreads itself, with almost no ad spend.
Run it: make the doorstep and the install so good that customers refer neighbors. Build a referral ask into every happy close — referrals are free, compounding reach.
Palantir Embed / mission
Palantir embedded "forward-deployed" people directly with customers to solve real problems and earn trust — wrapped in a clear mission, and the long game over quick wins.
Run it: your reps are forward-deployed — in the neighborhood, solving a real problem face-to-face. Lead with mission (connecting and protecting homes) and prove value on the spot with the free availability check.
Oracle Sales culture / objections
Oracle built a relentless, well-trained, uncapped-commission sales culture — and Larry Ellison had reps bring him every objection so the team could answer them. Bold, confident positioning, with a free trial as the wedge.
Run it: collect every door objection and turn it into content — answer the top ten in ten short videos. Train relentlessly, pay uncapped, and use the free quote as the no-risk wedge.

Foundations

Principles & canon

The ideas that recur across Alan Weiss, the marketing canon, and how the biggest companies actually won — the durable rules underneath the daily tactics.

Alan Weiss — the consulting marketing playbook
Value-based fees Pricing
Price the outcome you create, never your time or hours. Raise the perceived value instead of lowering the fee, and charge for the client's improved future, not your deliverables.
Run it: sell the result — a connected, protected home — not the line items.
Market gravity Attraction
Attract buyers to you with a steady stream of content and thought leadership instead of chasing them. Become the obvious object of interest in your area.
Run it: your organic content engine is market gravity — keep it running daily.
The accelerant curve Offers
Offer easy, low-barrier entry points that pull people toward higher-value, higher-trust relationships, ending in unique offers only you have.
Run it: free availability check first, then service, then referrals and add-ons.
The economic buyer Access
Reach the one person who can authorize the decision without anyone else's approval, and agree up front on the objective and the value.
Run it: at the door, talk to the decision-maker, not just whoever answers.
Referrals first Growth
A peer endorsing a peer is the highest-quality marketing there is. Build a body of work and let satisfied customers spread it for you.
Run it: ask every happy customer for two neighbors who'd want the same.

What the giants did to win
BlackRock Platform + scale
Put risk management at the core, built a proprietary platform (Aladdin) that competitors now depend on, and scaled through bold acquisitions — buying the iShares ETF business in the 2009 crisis made it the largest asset manager in the world.
Lesson: own something hard to copy; move decisively when others can't.
Blackstone Reputation + focus
Leveraged reputation and relationships to raise a record first fund, then scaled — while deliberately expanding only into areas where it had a real edge, protecting its reputation.
Lesson: trust opens doors; stay in your lane of advantage.
Berkshire Hathaway Moats + patience
"A moat that must be continuously rebuilt will eventually be no moat at all." — Warren Buffett
Buy and build durable economic moats — brand and pricing power, switching costs, network effects, cost advantages from scale — allocate capital patiently, and think in decades.
Lesson: a real, durable advantage compounds; chase durability, not hype.
Enduring greatness Fortune 500 research
Across the companies that lasted: humble-but-driven leaders, the right people first, brutal honesty about reality, and focus on the one thing you can be best at — run by a culture of discipline. Fortune-scale staples: clear vision, constant innovation, and keeping top talent.
Lesson: endurance depends more on what you do to yourself than on the market.
